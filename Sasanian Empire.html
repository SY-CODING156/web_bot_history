Sasanian Empire

The Second Persian Empire or (Sasanian Empire) is an empire that existed between the third and seventh centuries AD in the Middle East, where it was the only competitor to the Roman Empire and was going to destroy it. Ardashir I, or Ardashir Ibn Babak, Ibn Sasan, who was born in the year 180 AD in Tirudah from the villages of the city of Istakhr, is considered the founder of the Sasanian Empire after he overthrew the Parthian Empire in the year 224 AD The presence of the Sasanian Empire lasted for a period of 425 AD from the year 226 AD to 651 AD. It was the largest extension of the Sasanian Empire during the reign of King Khosrow II from the year 590 AD to 628 AD, exactly in the year 621 AD at the expense of the Byzantine Empire, and it completely collapsed in the year 651 AD at the hands of the Rashidun Caliphate, and its area was about 6,600,000 square kilometers
